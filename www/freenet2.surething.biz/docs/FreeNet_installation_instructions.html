FreeNet Software requirements and Installation Instructions

FreeNet is very easy to install since most of the work of installation is done by FreeNet over a remote connection to your server. FreeNet runs under Ubuntu Linux a completely free open source operating system. The software license for FreeNet is inclusive of all the other software needed to run on your FreeNet server including the operating system. No other software licenses to Microsoft or anyone else is needed. FreeNet is a completely turnkey operation with little or no effort.


Minimum hardware requirements to install and start a FreeNet minimal system.

1. Recommend a server PC computer with at least a 1.7 Ghz single core processor with 512 meg memory and at least a 20 gig available hard disk space. We have run on as small as 6 gig hard disk systems with 900 meg processors with only 64meg of memory but I'm not sure we still can support that with the present software we now run and we did run into problems with 64 meg memory that required reboots at times to correct (maybe once a month). You might want to provide a 300 gig hard disk or more to utilize the video on demand, music MP3 and other file sharing and PXE boot off LAN features that FreeNet has to offer. If you already have an old computer system laying around not being used it can't hurt to try it. If you can install Ubuntu 8.04 on it and it runs it will probably work for a FreeNet server.


2. A single ADSL Internet connection with minimum 100kB/sec Internet bandwidth from almost any ISP provider. We will recommend an ISP for you to use for the aria you are in and instruct you on how to get it if you don't already have one.


3. A single wifi accespoint to connect your network to the world. I guess this could be an option if you ran a wire only network but wifi is also a good way to self advertise and brings in new customers.


Optional added hardware and tools

4. We highly recommend a UPS (uninterpretable power supply) to prevent short power spikes from disrupting your service and damaging equipment. With installations of 10 or more customers this is mandatory to meet FreeNet minimal specifications. In most cases when a site grows to 10 or more customers in size we send a local FreeNet representative to visit the site to audit and check your installation for minimal requirements and specifications.


5. A 10+ port Ethernet switch hub is an optional addition but will be needed in wired systems. Most ADSL routers already provide a few extra Ethernet ports (4 to 5 ports) that you can use to start a small wired network.


6. 100 meters more or less of Cat5 cable and some tools including connector crimper, wire cutters, cable tester, drill, ladder. All this is optional if you want to hire a contractor to do your new cable runs or you just want to remain small with just a single wifi access point. You could later become a contractor for other FreeNet installations if you want. Just give FreeNet a call and we will add you to our list of possible contractors after you complete FreeNet installation certification. You should have at least a cable tester to verify and isolate troubled wire installations they are only 300 baht and will save you time and make sure that your contractors did there job before they leave. All FreeNet certified installers will have one .


List of FreeNet software features

1. User online self registration. A user can connect and start using FreeNet without any contact with a human even without a credit card. The local FreeNet admin (you) will be notified when a new user starts and you can contact them and collect your money when you have the time to do so. If the customer uses a credit card the admin does nothing, it's all automatic. Your paypal account will receive there payments. You can use your PayPal ATM card to get your money when you want to spend it or transfer the funds to your local bank account at some point. The Credit or paypal payment system is optional if you want to run a cash only system it's up to you.


2. User tracking and accounting. FreeNet keeps track of customer payments with auto user expiration. When the users prepaid account runs out of money the user is disabled access to the Internet and provided with renew options.


3. FreeNet store to buy different packages of FreeNet Internet access time with multiple methods of payment from credit card to cash all up to the customer how he wants to pay or how the local admin wants to receive his money. From 1 day to 1 year time windows are available to the customer to provide only what the customer needs or wants. You can add your own custom products or services as you wish but you must have the minimal FreeNet packages available to be called a FreeNet site.


4. FreeNet also has a local site forum that can be accessed by users without membership to provide customers or possible future customers a way to feedback system problems and for the FreeNet admin to communicate changes in the networks status. The forum can also be modified to add custom local interest feedback like crime activity local social fun events (parties) or anything of interest in the local surroundings. New subject addition can be opened to let the users/customers decide what forums they want to add or support.


5. FreeNet local network online classified add system to advertise goods for sale or a local apartments for rent in the building.


6. FreeNet local network auction site. Users can sell there goods or services on the a local auction like ebay.com.


7. File sharing over the local network from the FreeNet server using Samba can be implemented that enables Video on demand movies, music mp3 files or any application or document can be shared over the local LAN at transfer speeds of up to 100 megabyte per second or more. Users can read or write to the shared system if the admin (you) desires.


8. VPN virtual protected network service from anywhere in the world you can access your local FreeNet server network with the same security you have as if you were connected by wire. Useful when you are using wifi locally or at a untrusted remote wifi site to do security critical activities or just don't want people to know what your looking at or to bypass some site restrictions. Presently we use Openvpn with 1024bit RSA encryption key pears. Equivalent to SSL encryption you use to communicate with your bank with your browser.


9. FXE boot off LAN for wire customers only. Enables users to try out several operating systems on there user console without ever needing to install it on there local hard disk. Included boot off LAN operating systems Ubuntu 8.04, Ubuntu 9.1, DSL (dam small Linux) and more. Inside the Ubuntu 8.04 and 9.1 we also have Virtualbox that enables the user to run windows XP, Vista, window 7 and other systems within the security of the FXE booted Ubuntu systems. This includes file persistence so when you or the user log out or turn off there computers, files and changes will remain on the next system that boots into this private FXE system account. This can be useful to isolate user virus problems or to verify that a user has a hardware problem on there user console computer or just a configuration problem. You will later find that the more users that use Ubuntu Linux the less problems they and you will have. Most of the problems we receive are due to users with virus laden or mis configured Windows systems. FXE provides a try before you install to promote users to move to a better operating system.


10. Remote server software installation and upgrade and diagnostics provided by FreeNet and/or the admin.


11. Remote desktop access to enable remote training from online FreeNet instruction or to aid in isolating problems that a user, admin or customer may have.


12. scheduled single point bittorent download services to prevent disrupting shared bandwidth resources. Bittorrents downloads are scheduled when and at a rate that keeps the other customers from ever noticing any degradation in performance to the network.


These are just some of the more popular features. The list is endless and FreeNet is adding new features every day. As the base of users grows and feeds back what they want to change FreeNet will be there to give customers whatever they might desire. FreeNet is a glimpse of what many will see in the future but you will be a part of that future today. We are only limited by our combined imaginations.


Summery of FreeNet software remote installation


1. download a copy of Ubuntu 8.04 and install it on the computer you have selected as your FreeNet server. This software is completely free. Search with Google to find the download site needed to download the ISO file needed to create a boot CD.


2. Burn a CD of the downloaded Ubuntu 8.04 ISO file onto a CD that will be used as the boot install disk for your FreeNet server. If you use Windows you might try the program called Nero to burn the ISO to CD or whatever you normally us to burn to blank Cd's..


3. Boot up your FreeNet server with the Ubuntu boot CD from step 2. The hard disk on your new server will be reformatted in the installation so make sure you won't be losing something you left on the server drive for they shall all be lost after installation.


4. Select to install Ubuntu onto the hard disk of your new server. You will need to have the server connected to the Internet with an ADSL high speed connection during the installation.


5. Do a post checkout of the installed Ubuntu operating system to verify that you have an Internet connection and feel free to play with the Desktop if you like to get know and learn about your new toy.


6. Start the firefox browser and enter in the address box: http://freenet.surething.selfip.com/vpn/setup_freenet.sh select save file


7. open a terminal window from Applications>Accessories>Terminal in the new open terminal window :$sudo sh ./Downloads/setup_freenet.sh or wherever path you chose to save the file in step 6.


8. Inform FreeNet that you are now ready to install your FreeNet software and provide FreeNet . with what the admin password of the ADSL box that is connected to your FreeNet server that will be used for the ISP of your network.




9. wait for a call from FreeNet to inform you that the install has gone OK. If something went wrong FreeNet will inform you over the phone of what will need to be changed in the network hardware or software settings before it becomes operational. If the local admin can't solve the problem, FreeNet will schedule a n engineer to visit the site to find the problem and issue needed instructions to the local staff or contact contractors if needed. This should be done within 24 hours but if we have a flood of new installs it may take longer. Just leave the computer turned on and connected to the Internet it's should all automagicly install.


10. After the FreeNet contact in step 10, Try to connect to your new FreeNet wifi site with another computer to verify you see a login screen when you try to access the Internet with your test user computer.


11. Setup a test user account on the FreeNet self registration form and verify after registration and network login that your new user has access to the Internet. If you have problems here FreeNet may be able to help you with remote Desktop to show you what needs to be done. It shouldn't be difficult but if you have a question just ask FreeNet.


12. That's it now you just wait for new users to connect and start calling you asking you to accept there money to become members of your new wifi and/or wire network. You may want to start advertising with signs in the lobby of your building but it is not mandatory. When people see the accespoint on wifi with the name “FreeNet” they will know that's were they want to go for the best Internet connection they will ever find at a price they can afford.


13. Oh I did forget to tell you that FreeNet is an expandable system it can grow as you need it and you will be informed by FreeNet when we detect you need more. You can also grow yourself at any rate you wish but you will be required to meet FreeNet minimal performance requirements. We make sure that your customers will receive what they expect from FreeNet. Expansion can be as little as calling your ISP provider to increase bandwidth or adding a backup redundant ADSL line. A single server can provide up to 200 users or more but may require some added hard disk space at some point in time and backup redundant servers and more ISP provider lines can also be added in larger installations.
